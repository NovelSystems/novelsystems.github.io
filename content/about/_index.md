## Samuel C. Ryan — Principal Engineer

Systems engineer with industry experience in semiconductor device physics, optoelectronic component test, defense manufacturing compliance, and utility-scale flow battery test infrastructure. Prior roles include optoelectronic test engineering at Voxtel, thermal imaging camera manufacturing and DOD compliance programs at FLIR Systems, and test infrastructure development for iron-flow batteries at ESS Inc. I hold an M.Sc. in Semiconductor Process Engineering and a B.Sc. in Chemistry from the University of Oregon, and served as a wideband satellite communications specialist in the Oregon Air National Guard.

Novel Systems Engineering was founded to develop DDR sensor technology from first principles and build the patent portfolio around it. DDR (differential depolarization response) is a polarimetric measurement method that classifies materials by how they scatter polarized light. The licensing model follows from that directly: the work is the IP, not the hardware.
