IP Portfolio

IP portfolio summary

Novel Systems Engineering holds two provisional patent applications and is preparing four utility patent applications covering the DDR polarimetric LiDAR measurement method and related system implementations. The company licenses intellectual property. It does not manufacture sensors.

The portfolio covers the DDR measurement method itself, the specific sensor architecture used to perform DDR measurements, and application-level system designs built on DDR sensing. FMCW ranging and silicon photonic phased arrays are established techniques used in the sensor design. The novel claims relate to the DDR measurement principle, the dual orthogonal 1D array configuration as applied to DDR sensing, and the specific system-level combinations described in the applications.

Provisional applications establish a priority date. They do not grant patent rights and have not been examined. The four utility applications listed below are in draft and have not been filed. Nothing in this portfolio is an issued patent.

Provisional Applications Filed

Core DDR Measurement System

U.S. Provisional Application No. 63/984,145 · Filed February 16, 2026

Measures the difference in how a target depolarizes two orthogonal laser illumination states. That differential signal distinguishes materials that look identical to conventional LiDAR or radar.

Covers the DDR measurement method: differential scalar measurement of depolarization ratio asymmetry under orthogonal illumination states. The described implementation incorporates a dual orthogonal 1D silicon photonic phased array architecture, 1550 nm coherent detection, a four-channel polarimetric receiver, and FMCW ranging. The novel contribution is the DDR measurement approach and its integration with these components. FMCW ranging and silicon photonic phased arrays are prior art used within the system.

Provisional Filed

DDR System Extensions: Calibration, Acquisition, Tracking, and Response

U.S. Provisional Application · Filed approximately February 27, 2026

Four inventions that extend the core DDR system: self-calibration against environmental drift, automated target finding and beam steering, coordinated multi-sensor tracking, and triggered responses to classification results.

This provisional covers four inventions:

  • Adaptive environmental calibration. Method for compensating environmental effects on DDR measurements in real time.
  • Shared-aperture autonomous target acquisition. Closed-loop pointing control with adaptive field of view, using the DDR sensor aperture for both search and classification.
  • Multi-node drift-predictive tracking network. Coordinated handoff and predictive track maintenance across multiple DDR sensor nodes.
  • Automated classification response actions. System-level responses triggered by DDR classification outputs.
