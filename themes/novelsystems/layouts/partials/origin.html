The Problem

Designed for the hardest detection problem at sea

Search and rescue crews recovering persons in water face a fundamental sensor problem: radar and thermal imaging cannot reliably distinguish a swimmer from the rough sea surface around them, and the difference between a detection and a miss can be measured in minutes.

DDR was developed to solve that specific problem — not by improving existing sensor modalities, but by measuring a physical property that ocean water simply does not share with biological tissue.

Ocean water depolarizes light isotropically, producing a near-zero DDR signature. Biological tissue — skin, wetsuit, life vest — exhibits strong asymmetric scattering due to its layered, anisotropic structure. The result is a 20:1 contrast ratio that no sea state can wash out.
