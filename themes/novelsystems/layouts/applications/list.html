Licensing Domains

Three markets, one measurement principle

DDR is a platform measurement, not a point solution. The same polarimetric asymmetry that identifies a person in water also distinguishes drone airframes from birds and adds material classification to radar installations — addressed through separate licensing tracks.

Maritime

FMCW Maritime LiDAR

Shipboard and airborne person-in-water (PIW) detection is the primary DDR application domain. Current SAR technology relies on radar, thermal imaging, and visual search — none of which can reliably distinguish a floating person from wave crests and spray at operationally useful ranges in high sea states.

DDR-enabled maritime LiDAR operates at 3–5km slant range with automated classification output. No trained operator is required to interpret sensor returns; the system outputs a binary detection flag with associated position data for handoff to rescue crews. The 1550nm wavelength is eye-safe at all operational ranges, permitting unrestricted use near personnel.

The system is designed for integration into shipboard sensor mast installations and helicopter-mounted sensor pods. Licensing is available for both platform types under separate royalty structures.

Operating Parameters

  • Detection range: 300m – 5km slant
  • Sea state ceiling: Beaufort 4 validated, Beaufort 6 target
  • Target: persons in water, life rafts, survival suits
  • Output: binary flag + position, no image required
  • Wavelength: 1550nm (Class 1M, eye-safe)
  • Platform: shipboard mast or helicopter pod
Drone Detection

FMCW Drone Detection

Small UAS detection against low clutter airspace backgrounds presents a measurement problem structurally similar to PIW detection: the target's radar cross-section is small and variable, thermal contrast is low, and visual identification requires trained operators and favorable lighting.

DDR provides a material-discriminating channel that works against an airspace background. Composite carbon fiber and fiberglass drone airframes produce strong DDR signatures — the layered anisotropic structure of the materials drives high polarization asymmetry. Birds, balloons, and airspace clutter do not share this property.

The detection scheme operates against a near-zero atmospheric background, unlike maritime applications where ocean surface scatter provides a cluttered baseline. Atmospheric DDR background is effectively zero at 1550nm under typical operating conditions, permitting classification thresholds one order of magnitude more sensitive than the maritime case.

Operating Parameters

  • Detection range: 200m – 2km (small UAS)
  • Target: fixed-wing and multirotor UAS, composite airframes
  • Background: near-zero atmospheric DDR
  • Discrimination: UAS vs birds vs weather clutter
  • Output: classification flag + track initiation
  • Integration: C-UAS sensor fusion layer
Radar Augmentation

Pulsed ToF Radar Augmentation

Conventional maritime and coastal surveillance radar installations cannot classify targets by material composition — they can detect and track, but cannot distinguish between a life raft, a shipping container, a buoy, and a swimmer at the same range and radar cross-section.

DDR augmentation adds a polarimetric overlay channel to existing radar apertures without requiring hardware replacement. The augmentation system uses a pulsed time-of-flight (ToF) LiDAR operating in the same pointing direction as the host radar, synchronized to the radar's scan pattern. Returns are fused in the radar's track management layer to add a material classification attribute to each tracked contact.

This licensing track targets existing radar installations at coast guard stations, offshore platforms, port facilities, and naval vessels. The augmentation package integrates through a standard RS-422 or Ethernet data interface.

Operating Parameters

  • Ranging: pulsed ToF, synchronized to host radar
  • Classification: material DDR overlay per radar contact
  • Integration: RS-422 or Ethernet, track-layer fusion
  • Hardware: add-on aperture, no radar modification
  • Target platforms: coast guard, offshore, port, naval
  • Range: matched to host radar detection envelope
